Trip Snapshot
Seasonal Overview – Autumn (March–April): Autumn – one of the loveliest times to tour New Zealand. Expect settled, mild days (14–22°C), gorgeous light and far thinner crowds than summer. Your timing is ideal for Central Otago and Wanaka's golden autumn colour (early–mid April is peak) and for wine-harvest season in Hawke's Bay and Marlborough. Pack warm layers for the alpine stops (Tongariro, Aoraki/Mt Cook) and a rain shell for the West Coast – daylight shortens to roughly 7am–7pm by mid-April.

Day 14 · Sun 28 Mar 2027 🌙 2 nights

🚗 Drive🇳🇿 NZ
A short, scenic hop brings you to Blenheim – the beating heart of Marlborough, New Zealand's largest and most famous wine region. Two nights to taste, cycle the vineyards, and discover the WWI aviation treasures at the Omaka Heritage Centre.

Day 30 · Tue 13 Apr 2027 🌙 2 nights
Mount Cook → Christchurch (Inland Scenic Route)
🚗 Drive🇳🇿 NZ
Your final cross-country drive crosses the Canterbury high country and braided rivers to Christchurch – the Garden City. Reinvented since the earthquakes, it blends heritage trams and punted rivers with bold street art and a brilliant food scene. Two nights to explore the city, the Banks Peninsula and Akaroa before your journey home.
Scenic stops along the way
  • Lake Tekapo & Church of the Good Shepherd – Turquoise lake & NZ's most photographed chapel
  • Burkes Pass & Fairlie – Country-town pie stop – a Mackenzie institution
  • Rakaia Gorge – Braided-river canyon on the inland approach to the plains
